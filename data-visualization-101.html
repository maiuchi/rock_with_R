2 Data visualization 101

What is data visualization?

  • "A visual way to tell a story with data and information" (Sosulki, 2019)
  • "A kind of narrative, providing a clear answer to a question without extraneous details" (Ware, 2008)
  • "The representation and presentation of data to facilitate understanding" (Kirk, 2016)

This is what I think...
Data visualization is a tool to translate data into an interesting story that is attractive, accurate, and easy to understand

Why visualization matters?

Visualization matters, because it is the means to communicate effectively with your audience. Even if you got an awesome finding in your research, no one but you could understand what it means or why it is important, your research might miss the moment to shine.

What makes it a good visualization?

Your audience understand the message you want to convery from visuals effortlessly

When you make a data vis piece, think:

  • about medium
    • are you presenting your visuals on a presentation slide, report, or interactive web?
  • about objectives
    • what is the purpose of making your visuals?
  • who your audience are
    • do they have field knowledge? Or, general audience?
  • about a story you want to tell
    • what do you want to convey the most from your visualization piece?
    • what do you want to highlight the most from your visualization piece?
  • what you can omit
    • is there anything that I can omit?
  • about colors
    • do not mix lots of colors
      • unless you have a good control in colors, I would choose 1 or 2 colors or play with the shades to make it look uniformed
  • about white space
    • cluttered graphs are hard to read
  • about accuracy
    • sometimes you need to compromise a bit of accuracy due to visualization purposes
      • be mindful of the risks and make sure that your visuals are not misrepresenting the results (exaggerating/ minimizing)
