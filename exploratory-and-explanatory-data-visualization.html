3 Exploratory and explanatory data visualization

Exploratory visualization

An effective tool to "help us understand the data" (DataCamp, 2019)

Anscombe's Quartet is a classic example that depicts why exploratory visualization is important. This concept is demonstrated by a statistician Francis Anscombe. The dataset consists as follows:

When you calculate some of the descriptive statistics, you will soon notice that those are pretty much exactly the same.

However, when we visualize this data, we know that their distributions are quite different:

This highlights the reason as to why exploratory visualization is important! When you conduct any kind of data analysis, we usually visualize data first to make sure the distributions. The purpose of exploratory data visualization is to understand the data better. Therefore, no need to spend time to make visuals clean and digestible for others.

Explanatory visualization

An effective tool to communicate with others

Unlike exploratory analysis, explanatory analysis comes to the last part of your data analysis project. The purpose of explanatory visualization is to communicate with others regarding your final results.

When you make an explanatory visualization piece, make sure to think about the elements in What makes it a good visualization? The golden rules for explanatory visualization I think are: clean, as accurate as possible, and easy to understand.

Also, there are types of graphs that are suitable to visualize depending on data types and patterns. For instance, if you are making an explanatory visualization piece for a time-trend analysis, line graphs are good ones to use.

Here is a webpage that showcases types of graph you can use. I understand that complex graphs are usually more visually appealing. However, please keep in mind that those usually require some type of design skills to make. Also, your audience need to study the graphs to understand. Therefore, think which occasion, medium, and audience will be appropriate to make more complex graphs.

One last thing... when you make explanatory visualization pieces, you will often need to reformat/ recreate datasets. This means that the process might take longer than you think to visualize your data effectively.
